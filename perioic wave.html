PERIODIC WAVES

A periodic wave is a repeating wave that maintains a consistent pattern over time, characterized by a constant wavelength and frequency. These waves exhibit a clear cycle of motion that repeats itself. Periodic Waves | Definition & Examples Here's a more detailed breakdown:

  1. Repetition: Periodic waves are defined by their repeating pattern, meaning the same wave shape appears repeatedly.
  2. Constant Characteristics: The key characteristics of a periodic wave, like wavelength and frequency, remain constant throughout the wave's propagation.
  3. Wavelength: The distance between two consecutive peaks or troughs in the wave pattern.
  4. Frequency: The number of complete cycles (or repetitions) of the wave that occur in a given time period.
  5. Amplitude: The maximum displacement of a point on the wave from its resting position.
  6. Period: The time required for one complete cycle of the wave.
